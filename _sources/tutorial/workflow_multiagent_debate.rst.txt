Multi-Agent Debate
========================

Debate workflow simulates a multi-turn discussion between different agents, mostly several solvers and an aggregator.
Typically, the solvers generate and exchange their answers, while the aggregator collects and summarizes the answers.

We implement the examples in `EMNLP 2024`_, where two debater agents will discuss a topic in a fixed order, and express their
arguments based on the previous debate history.
At each round a moderator agent will decide whether the correct answer can be obtained in the current iteration.

    import asyncio
    import os

    from pydantic import Field, BaseModel

    from agentscope.agent import ReActAgent
    from agentscope.formatter import (
        DashScopeMultiAgentFormatter,
        DashScopeChatFormatter,
    )
    from agentscope.message import Msg
    from agentscope.model import DashScopeChatModel
    from agentscope.pipeline import MsgHub

    # Prepare a topic
    topic = (
        "The two circles are externally tangent and there is no relative sliding. "
        "The radius of circle A is 1/3 the radius of circle B. Circle A rolls "
        "around circle B one trip back to its starting point. How many times will "
        "circle A revolve in total?"
    )


    # Create two debater agents, Alice and Bob, who will discuss the topic.
    def create_solver_agent(name: str) -> ReActAgent:
        """Get a solver agent."""
        return ReActAgent(
            name=name,
            sys_prompt=f"You're a debater named {name}. Hello and welcome to the "
            "debate competition. It's unnecessary to fully agree with "
            "each other's perspectives, as our objective is to find "
            "the correct answer. The debate topic is stated as "
            f"follows: {topic}.",
            model=DashScopeChatModel(
                model_name="qwen-max",
                api_key=os.environ["DASHSCOPE_API_KEY"],
                stream=False,
            ),
            formatter=DashScopeMultiAgentFormatter(),
        )


    alice, bob = [create_solver_agent(name) for name in ["Alice", "Bob"]]

    # Create a moderator agent
    moderator = ReActAgent(
        name="Aggregator",
        sys_prompt=f"""You're a moderator. There will be two debaters involved in a debate competition. They will present their answer and discuss their perspectives on the topic:
    ``````
    {topic}
    ``````
    At the end of each round, you will evaluate both sides' answers and decide which one is correct.""",
        model=DashScopeChatModel(
            model_name="qwen-max",
            api_key=os.environ["DASHSCOPE_API_KEY"],
            stream=False,
        ),
        # Use multiagent formatter because the moderator will receive messages from more than a user and an assistant
        formatter=DashScopeMultiAgentFormatter(),
    )


    # A structured output model for the moderator
    class JudgeModel(BaseModel):
        """The structured output model for the moderator."""

        finished: bool = Field(
            description="Whether the debate is finished.",
        )
        correct_answer: str | None = Field(
            description="The correct answer to the debate topic, only if the debate is finished. Otherwise, leave it as None.",
            default=None,
        )


    async def run_multiagent_debate() -> None:
        """Run the multi-agent debate workflow."""
        while True:
            # The reply messages in MsgHub from the participants will be broadcasted to all participants.
            async with MsgHub(participants=[alice, bob, moderator]):
                await alice(
                    Msg(
                        "user",
                        "You are affirmative side, Please express your viewpoints.",
                        "user",
                    ),
                )
                await bob(
                    Msg(
                        "user",
                        "You are negative side. You disagree with the affirmative side. Provide your reason and answer.",
                        "user",
                    ),
                )

            # Alice and Bob doesn't need to know the moderator's message, so moderator is called outside the MsgHub.
            msg_judge = await moderator(
                Msg(
                    "user",
                    "Now you have heard the answers from the others, have the debate finished, and can you get the correct answer?",
                    "user",
                ),
                structured_model=JudgeModel,
            )

            if msg_judge.metadata.get("finished"):
                print(
                    "\nThe debate is finished, and the correct answer is: ",
                    msg_judge.metadata.get("correct_answer"),
                )
                break


    asyncio.run(run_multiagent_debate())

    Alice: Thank you. As the affirmative side, I will present the argument that when circle A, with a radius 1/3 that of circle B, rolls around circle B one complete trip back to its starting point, it will revolve 4 times in total.

    To understand this, let's denote the radius of circle B as \( R \) and the radius of circle A as \( r = \frac{R}{3} \).

    When circle A rolls around circle B without slipping, the distance traveled by the center of circle A is equal to the circumference of the path it follows. This path is a circle with a radius equal to the sum of the radii of circles A and B, which is \( R + r = R + \frac{R}{3} = \frac{4R}{3} \). The circumference of this path is therefore \( 2\pi \times \frac{4R}{3} = \frac{8\pi R}{3} \).

    Since there is no relative sliding, the distance traveled by the center of circle A is also the distance that the circumference of circle A has rolled. The circumference of circle A is \( 2\pi r = 2\pi \times \frac{R}{3} = \frac{2\pi R}{3} \).

    The number of revolutions that circle A makes is the total distance traveled by its center divided by the circumference of circle A:

    \[ \text{Number of Revolutions} = \frac{\frac{8\pi R}{3}}{\frac{2\pi R}{3}} = \frac{8\pi R}{3} \times \frac{3}{2\pi R} = 4 \]

    Therefore, circle A will revolve 4 times in total as it rolls around circle B.
    Bob: Thank you. As the negative side, I will present an argument that challenges the conclusion of 4 revolutions and suggests a different number of revolutions for circle A as it rolls around circle B.

    Firstly, let's agree on the setup: The radius of circle A is \( r = \frac{R}{3} \) and the radius of circle B is \( R \). When circle A rolls around circle B, the path traced by the center of circle A is indeed a circle with a radius of \( R + r = \frac{4R}{3} \), and the circumference of this path is \( \frac{8\pi R}{3} \).

    However, the key point to consider is not just the distance traveled by the center of circle A, but also the relative rotation of circle A with respect to its own center. When circle A rolls without slipping, each point on the circumference of circle A touches the surface of the path once per revolution. 

    As circle A completes one full trip around circle B, it must also rotate due to the curvature of the path. For every complete circuit, the center of circle A travels a distance equal to the circumference of the path, which is \( \frac{8\pi R}{3} \). Since the circumference of circle A is \( \frac{2\pi R}{3} \), we can calculate the number of times the circumference of circle A fits into the path:

    \[ \text{Number of Revolutions (relative to the path)} = \frac{\frac{8\pi R}{3}}{\frac{2\pi R}{3}} = 4 \]

    This calculation shows that circle A makes 4 revolutions relative to the path. However, we must also account for the fact that as circle A moves around circle B, it also rotates around its own center. This additional rotation is due to the change in direction of the velocity vector of any point on the circumference of circle A, which is equivalent to one full rotation as it completes the loop around circle B.

    Thus, the total number of revolutions made by circle A is the sum of the revolutions relative to the path and the additional rotation due to the change in direction:

    \[ \text{Total Number of Revolutions} = 4 + 1 = 5 \]

    Therefore, my argument is that circle A will revolve 5 times in total as it rolls around circle B.
