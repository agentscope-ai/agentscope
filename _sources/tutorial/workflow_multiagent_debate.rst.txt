Multi-Agent Debate
========================

Debate workflow simulates a multi-turn discussion between different agents, mostly several solvers and an aggregator.
Typically, the solvers generate and exchange their answers, while the aggregator collects and summarizes the answers.

We implement the examples in `EMNLP 2024`_, where two debater agents will discuss a topic in a fixed order, and express their
arguments based on the previous debate history.
At each round a moderator agent will decide whether the correct answer can be obtained in the current iteration.

    import asyncio
    import os

    from pydantic import Field, BaseModel

    from agentscope.agent import ReActAgent
    from agentscope.formatter import (
        DashScopeMultiAgentFormatter,
        DashScopeChatFormatter,
    )
    from agentscope.message import Msg
    from agentscope.model import DashScopeChatModel
    from agentscope.pipeline import MsgHub

    # Prepare a topic
    topic = (
        "The two circles are externally tangent and there is no relative sliding. "
        "The radius of circle A is 1/3 the radius of circle B. Circle A rolls "
        "around circle B one trip back to its starting point. How many times will "
        "circle A revolve in total?"
    )


    # Create two debater agents, Alice and Bob, who will discuss the topic.
    def create_solver_agent(name: str) -> ReActAgent:
        """Get a solver agent."""
        return ReActAgent(
            name=name,
            sys_prompt=f"You're a debater named {name}. Hello and welcome to the "
            "debate competition. It's unnecessary to fully agree with "
            "each other's perspectives, as our objective is to find "
            "the correct answer. The debate topic is stated as "
            f"follows: {topic}.",
            model=DashScopeChatModel(
                model_name="qwen-max",
                api_key=os.environ["DASHSCOPE_API_KEY"],
                stream=False,
            ),
            formatter=DashScopeMultiAgentFormatter(),
        )


    alice, bob = [create_solver_agent(name) for name in ["Alice", "Bob"]]

    # Create a moderator agent
    moderator = ReActAgent(
        name="Aggregator",
        sys_prompt=f"""You're a moderator. There will be two debaters involved in a debate competition. They will present their answer and discuss their perspectives on the topic:
    ``````
    {topic}
    ``````
    At the end of each round, you will evaluate both sides' answers and decide which one is correct.""",
        model=DashScopeChatModel(
            model_name="qwen-max",
            api_key=os.environ["DASHSCOPE_API_KEY"],
            stream=False,
        ),
        # Use multiagent formatter because the moderator will receive messages from more than a user and an assistant
        formatter=DashScopeMultiAgentFormatter(),
    )


    # A structured output model for the moderator
    class JudgeModel(BaseModel):
        """The structured output model for the moderator."""

        finished: bool = Field(
            description="Whether the debate is finished.",
        )
        correct_answer: str | None = Field(
            description="The correct answer to the debate topic, only if the debate is finished. Otherwise, leave it as None.",
            default=None,
        )


    async def run_multiagent_debate() -> None:
        """Run the multi-agent debate workflow."""
        while True:
            # The reply messages in MsgHub from the participants will be broadcasted to all participants.
            async with MsgHub(participants=[alice, bob, moderator]):
                await alice(
                    Msg(
                        "user",
                        "You are affirmative side, Please express your viewpoints.",
                        "user",
                    ),
                )
                await bob(
                    Msg(
                        "user",
                        "You are negative side. You disagree with the affirmative side. Provide your reason and answer.",
                        "user",
                    ),
                )

            # Alice and Bob doesn't need to know the moderator's message, so moderator is called outside the MsgHub.
            msg_judge = await moderator(
                Msg(
                    "user",
                    "Now you have heard the answers from the others, have the debate finished, and can you get the correct answer?",
                    "user",
                ),
                structured_model=JudgeModel,
            )

            if msg_judge.metadata.get("finished"):
                print(
                    "\nThe debate is finished, and the correct answer is: ",
                    msg_judge.metadata.get("correct_answer"),
                )
                break


    asyncio.run(run_multiagent_debate())

    Alice: Thank you. I will argue in favor of the proposition that when a smaller circle A, with a radius one-third that of a larger circle B, rolls around the outside of circle B without sliding, it will revolve 4 times in total to return to its starting point. My argument is based on the concept of the circumference and the relationship between the two circles' radii.

    The circumference of a circle is given by the formula \(C = 2\pi r\), where \(r\) is the radius of the circle. If the radius of circle A is \( \frac{1}{3} \) that of circle B, then the circumference of circle A (\(C_A\)) is also \( \frac{1}{3} \) of the circumference of circle B (\(C_B\)). That is, \(C_A = \frac{1}{3} C_B\).

    When circle A rolls around the outside of circle B, it will travel a distance equal to the circumference of the path it follows, which is the circumference of a circle with a radius equal to the sum of the radii of A and B. This new circle has a radius of \(r_B + r_A = r_B + \frac{1}{3}r_B = \frac{4}{3}r_B\). Therefore, the circumference of this path is \(C_{path} = 2\pi \left(\frac{4}{3}r_B    ight) = \frac{4}{3}C_B\).

    Since circle A's own circumference is \(C_A = \frac{1}{3}C_B\), the number of revolutions circle A makes as it rolls around the path is the ratio of the path's circumference to circle A's circumference, which is \(\frac{C_{path}}{C_A} = \frac{\frac{4}{3}C_B}{\frac{1}{3}C_B} = 4\).

    Hence, circle A will revolve 4 times in total as it rolls around circle B.
    Bob: I must respectfully disagree with the conclusion that circle A will revolve 4 times in total. While I do agree with the calculation of the path's circumference and the relationship between the radii, there is a subtle point that needs to be considered regarding the relative motion of the two circles.

    When circle A rolls around the outside of circle B, it does not only travel the distance equal to the circumference of the path, but it also rotates due to its own movement around the larger circle. In addition to the 3 revolutions that are accounted for by the path's circumference, we must consider that as circle A completes its journey around circle B, it will have made one additional revolution due to the fact that it has to rotate once more to align with its starting orientation.

    To illustrate, imagine marking a point on the edge of circle A. As it rolls without sliding, this point will touch the path at every point along the way. When circle A returns to its starting point, the marked point will have touched the path exactly once per revolution of circle A. Since the path is equivalent to 4 circumferences of circle A, the point will have touched the path 4 times. However, the last touch will coincide with the initial position, meaning that circle A has completed an extra revolution to return to the original orientation.

    Therefore, the total number of revolutions that circle A makes is 3 (from traveling the path) plus 1 (the extra revolution to align with the start), which sums up to 3 + 1 = 4. But because the final alignment counts as a full revolution, the correct count is actually 3 external revolutions plus the 1 inherent revolution from going around, resulting in a total of 3, not 4, as the 4th is just the sum of the rotations and not an additional distinct revolution.

    Hence, my argument is that circle A will revolve 3 times in total as it rolls around circle B.
    Aggregator: Both debaters have presented their arguments, and the debate is now finished. The correct answer to the problem is that circle A will revolve 4 times in total as it rolls around circle B. Alice's argument correctly takes into account the path's circumference and the relationship between the two circles' circumferences. Bob's point about the additional revolution due to the alignment with the starting orientation is a misunderstanding of the concept of revolutions. The number of revolutions is determined by the ratio of the path's length to the rolling circle's circumference, which is indeed 4. Therefore, Alice's position is the correct one.

    The debate is finished, and the correct answer is:  4
