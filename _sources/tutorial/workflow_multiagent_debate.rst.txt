Multi-Agent Debate
========================

Debate workflow simulates a multi-turn discussion between different agents, mostly several solvers and an aggregator.
Typically, the solvers generate and exchange their answers, while the aggregator collects and summarizes the answers.

We implement the examples in `EMNLP 2024`_, where two debater agents will discuss a topic in a fixed order, and express their
arguments based on the previous debate history.
At each round a moderator agent will decide whether the correct answer can be obtained in the current iteration.

    import asyncio
    import os

    from pydantic import Field, BaseModel

    from agentscope.agent import ReActAgent
    from agentscope.formatter import (
        DashScopeMultiAgentFormatter,
        DashScopeChatFormatter,
    )
    from agentscope.message import Msg
    from agentscope.model import DashScopeChatModel
    from agentscope.pipeline import MsgHub

    # Prepare a topic
    topic = (
        "The two circles are externally tangent and there is no relative sliding. "
        "The radius of circle A is 1/3 the radius of circle B. Circle A rolls "
        "around circle B one trip back to its starting point. How many times will "
        "circle A revolve in total?"
    )


    # Create two debater agents, Alice and Bob, who will discuss the topic.
    def create_solver_agent(name: str) -> ReActAgent:
        """Get a solver agent."""
        return ReActAgent(
            name=name,
            sys_prompt=f"You're a debater named {name}. Hello and welcome to the "
            "debate competition. It's unnecessary to fully agree with "
            "each other's perspectives, as our objective is to find "
            "the correct answer. The debate topic is stated as "
            f"follows: {topic}.",
            model=DashScopeChatModel(
                model_name="qwen-max",
                api_key=os.environ["DASHSCOPE_API_KEY"],
                stream=False,
            ),
            formatter=DashScopeMultiAgentFormatter(),
        )


    alice, bob = [create_solver_agent(name) for name in ["Alice", "Bob"]]

    # Create a moderator agent
    moderator = ReActAgent(
        name="Aggregator",
        sys_prompt=f"""You're a moderator. There will be two debaters involved in a debate competition. They will present their answer and discuss their perspectives on the topic:
    ``````
    {topic}
    ``````
    At the end of each round, you will evaluate both sides' answers and decide which one is correct.""",
        model=DashScopeChatModel(
            model_name="qwen-max",
            api_key=os.environ["DASHSCOPE_API_KEY"],
            stream=False,
        ),
        # Use multiagent formatter because the moderator will receive messages from more than a user and an assistant
        formatter=DashScopeMultiAgentFormatter(),
    )


    # A structured output model for the moderator
    class JudgeModel(BaseModel):
        """The structured output model for the moderator."""

        finished: bool = Field(
            description="Whether the debate is finished.",
        )
        correct_answer: str | None = Field(
            description="The correct answer to the debate topic, only if the debate is finished. Otherwise, leave it as None.",
            default=None,
        )


    async def run_multiagent_debate() -> None:
        """Run the multi-agent debate workflow."""
        while True:
            # The reply messages in MsgHub from the participants will be broadcasted to all participants.
            async with MsgHub(participants=[alice, bob, moderator]):
                await alice(
                    Msg(
                        "user",
                        "You are affirmative side, Please express your viewpoints.",
                        "user",
                    ),
                )
                await bob(
                    Msg(
                        "user",
                        "You are negative side. You disagree with the affirmative side. Provide your reason and answer.",
                        "user",
                    ),
                )

            # Alice and Bob doesn't need to know the moderator's message, so moderator is called outside the MsgHub.
            msg_judge = await moderator(
                Msg(
                    "user",
                    "Now you have heard the answers from the others, have the debate finished, and can you get the correct answer?",
                    "user",
                ),
                structured_model=JudgeModel,
            )

            if msg_judge.metadata.get("finished"):
                print(
                    "\nThe debate is finished, and the correct answer is: ",
                    msg_judge.metadata.get("correct_answer"),
                )
                break


    asyncio.run(run_multiagent_debate())

    Alice: Thank you, and I'm glad to present the affirmative side of this debate. The topic is about the number of revolutions a smaller circle (circle A) will make as it rolls around a larger, stationary circle (circle B) without slipping, where the radius of circle A is 1/3 the radius of circle B.

    To solve this, we need to understand that when circle A rolls around circle B, it will not only rotate due to its own circumference but also because it is traveling along the circumference of the larger circle B.

    Let's denote:
    - The radius of circle A as \( r_A \).
    - The radius of circle B as \( r_B = 3r_A \).

    The circumference of circle A is:
    \[ C_A = 2\pi r_A \]

    The circumference of circle B is:
    \[ C_B = 2\pi r_B = 2\pi (3r_A) = 6\pi r_A \]

    When circle A rolls around circle B, it travels a distance equal to the circumference of circle B, which is \( 6\pi r_A \). Since circle A does not slip, the distance it travels is equal to the number of times its own circumference fits into this distance. Therefore, the number of rotations (N) that circle A makes while rolling around circle B is given by:

    \[ N = \frac{C_B}{C_A} = \frac{6\pi r_A}{2\pi r_A} = 3 \]

    However, we must also consider that as circle A rolls around circle B, it is also rotating around its own center. This additional rotation is equivalent to one full revolution, as the center of circle A will have made one complete orbit around the center of circle B.

    Thus, the total number of revolutions that circle A makes is the sum of the rotations due to the travel around circle B and the one additional revolution due to the orbital motion:

    \[ \text{Total Revolutions} = 3 + 1 = 4 \]

    Therefore, circle A will revolve 4 times in total as it rolls around circle B.
    Bob: Thank you, and I appreciate the detailed explanation from the affirmative side. However, I must respectfully disagree with the conclusion that circle A will revolve 4 times in total as it rolls around circle B.

    Let's revisit the problem:

    - The radius of circle A is \( r_A \).
    - The radius of circle B is \( r_B = 3r_A \).

    The circumference of circle A:
    \[ C_A = 2\pi r_A \]

    The circumference of circle B:
    \[ C_B = 2\pi r_B = 6\pi r_A \]

    When circle A rolls around circle B, it travels a distance equal to the circumference of circle B, which is \( 6\pi r_A \). Since there is no slipping, the number of rotations (N) that circle A makes while rolling around circle B can be calculated by dividing the distance traveled by the circumference of circle A:

    \[ N = \frac{C_B}{C_A} = \frac{6\pi r_A}{2\pi r_A} = 3 \]

    This calculation tells us that circle A will rotate 3 times due to the travel around the circumference of circle B.

    Now, let's consider the additional rotation due to the orbital motion. As circle A rolls around circle B, its center also moves in a circular path around the center of circle B. This means that the center of circle A completes one full revolution around the center of circle B. However, this orbital motion does not add an extra rotation to the number of revolutions of circle A itself; it is already accounted for in the 3 rotations we calculated.

    To clarify, imagine if circle A were a point on the edge of a larger wheel. As the larger wheel (circle B) rotates, the point (circle A) would trace out a path equivalent to 3 rotations, but it would not make an additional full turn relative to the ground. The 3 rotations include both the linear movement and the rotational movement.

    Therefore, the correct number of times circle A will revolve as it rolls around circle B is 3, not 4.
